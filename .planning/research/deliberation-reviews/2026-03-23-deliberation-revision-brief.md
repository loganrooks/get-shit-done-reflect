# Deliberation Revision Brief

## What this pass is for

Revise the current deliberations using the now-stabilized review set. This is
not a roadmap-shaping pass yet. The goal is to improve the deliberations
themselves so later roadmap work rests on cleaner artifacts.

## Global constraints

- Keep deliberations open unless there is unusually strong reason not to.
- Do not overclaim from literature, signals, or review consensus.
- Preserve productive tensions instead of forcing harmony.
- Make uncertainty, insufficiency, and transfer limits explicit where needed.
- Do not let shared review conclusions read as independent confirmation.
- Do not turn interpretive or philosophical pressure into fake mechanism just
  because the artifact system prefers concrete outputs.
- Keep a distinction between:
  - diagnostic force
  - candidate design implications
  - framework commitments not yet warranted

## What to improve in general

- Clarify governance and scope where provenance or ownership is ambiguous.
- Make it easier to tell what is:
  - project-local evidence
  - framework-level implication
  - still only a candidate pattern
- Add or sharpen explicit cautions where a reader might mistake a live inquiry
  for an adopted conclusion.
- Preserve traces of how the inquiry developed when that development matters to
  the artifact's meaning, but avoid bloating the artifact with unnecessary
  repetition.

## File-specific revision aims

### 1. `spike-epistemic-rigor-and-framework-reflexivity.md`

- Fix governance/source-of-truth ambiguity.
- Remove or soften anything that makes framework status depend on old
  `arxiv-mcp` / `~/.claude/...` provenance accidents.
- Preserve the strong failure-chain diagnosis.
- Keep the narrowing learned from the reviews:
  thinner interventions first; no default assumption that more reviewer roles or
  broad dialogical redesign are already justified.
- Keep the artifact open to broader reflexivity questions without pretending the
  current evidence has already settled framework redesign.

### 2. `comparative-characterization-and-nonadditive-evaluation-praxis.md`

- Clarify more explicitly what is:
  - local methodology for `arxiv-sanity-mcp`
  - potentially portable framework pattern
  - still speculative
- Preserve the contribution around claim-local rigor, condition matrices, and
  non-additive revision.
- Avoid making claim cards or related structures sound like already-warranted
  framework apparatus.

### 3. `forms-excess-and-framework-becoming.md`

- Preserve its strength as diagnosis and artifact-ecology critique.
- Avoid pushing it into a tidy intervention program it does not warrant.
- Sharpen the distinction between:
  - what seems like framework pressure
  - what should remain open orientation

### 4. `responsibility-alterity-and-methodological-praxis.md`

- Preserve it as an interpretive orientation rather than a mechanism proposal.
- Make translation limits and uncertainties visible.
- Avoid operationalizing it into a checklist.

### 5. `community-feedback-pipelines-and-dialogue-forms.md`

- Make its later-stage status explicit.
- Keep it as orientation and design pressure, not near-term roadmap driver.
- Reflect present scale honestly: the framework is still used by a tiny core,
  so internal routing, traceability, and artifact discipline come first.

## Out of scope for this pass

- Do not patch `ROADMAP.md` yet.
- Do not create new milestone structure yet.
- Do not touch unrelated dirty files such as `.planning/config.json`.
