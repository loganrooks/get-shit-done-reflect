# Deliberation Constellation Review Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Scope:** Review of four Claude-authored framework deliberations in `get-shit-done-reflect`:
- `community-feedback-pipelines-and-dialogue-forms.md`
- `spike-epistemic-rigor-and-framework-reflexivity.md`
- `responsibility-alterity-and-methodological-praxis.md`
- `forms-excess-and-framework-becoming.md`

**Purpose:** Preserve the review frame and decision criteria across context resets before the substantive review is completed.

## Review Method

The review should not collapse into a single question like "is this a good idea?" It should run on multiple layers at once.

### Layer 1: Artifact Quality

Questions:
- Is the deliberation well-framed?
- Does it distinguish evidence from interpretation?
- Are open questions genuinely open, or disguised conclusions?
- Does the recommendation section match the actual state of the analysis?
- Does the artifact preserve enough of the inquiry path to be interpretable later?

Outputs:
- strengths
- findings / gaps
- specific missing evidence
- specific overclaims or premature closures

### Layer 2: Design Content

Questions:
- What concrete changes is the deliberation pointing toward?
- Does it imply changes to templates, workflows, artifact types, signals, review loops, community channels, or governance?
- Are the proposed moves actually implementable, or are they still orientation-level?
- What design options are being ignored?

Outputs:
- candidate interventions
- missing alternatives
- coupling and dependency risks

### Layer 3: Design Stance

Questions:
- What kind of design practice is this deliberation trying to install?
- Does it push the system toward closure, proceduralism, dialogue, revision, contestability, trace preservation, or some mix?
- What kinds of failure is it especially sensitive to?
- What kinds of failure is it in danger of missing?

Outputs:
- stance summary
- promise of the deliberation
- blind spots created by that stance

### Layer 4: Form Experiment

Questions:
- What does this artifact teach us about deliberation form itself?
- What exceeded the form?
- What had to be preserved in edit history, narrative trace, side comments, or sibling deliberations?
- What did the form flatten, over-polish, or separate too aggressively?
- Does this point to template changes, sidecar artifacts, trace logs, or new statuses?

Outputs:
- implications for deliberation template
- implications for trace/history preservation
- implications for artifact ecology, not just one artifact

### Layer 5: Program / Roadmap Effect

Questions:
- Should this modify an existing phase?
- Should it become a new phase, and where?
- Should it be deferred to the next milestone?
- Is it a local patch, a quick task, a phase, or a milestone-level reorientation?
- What dependencies would any such insertion have?

Outputs:
- roadmap placement recommendation
- dependency mapping
- defer / adopt / split recommendation

### Layer 6: Translation Layer

Questions:
- How do we translate the deeper orientation of the deliberation into technical and organizational language without neutralizing it?
- What does the deliberation mean in harness-design terms?
- What is the corresponding systems-design pressure?

Examples of translation:
- "attention to excluded others" -> design for excluded cases, absent users, and out-of-distribution situations
- "trace" -> preserve evidence of what the framework could not represent, not just what it concluded
- "resistance to premature closure" -> support reversible outcomes, deferred decisions, and qualified verdicts
- "dialogue" -> preserve challenge channels, reviewer questions, and interruption paths rather than only pass/fail gates

Outputs:
- translated design language
- risks of flattening the translation

### Layer 7: Apparatus-Change Layer

This layer is explicitly required. The review must not stop at "current GSD cannot hold this."

Questions:
- If the current apparatus cannot hold the deliberation's demand, how might it change in order to hold it?
- What concrete harness changes are possible?
- Should it change?
- At what cost?
- What are the alternatives to changing the apparatus?

This layer should present options, not a single inevitability.

Possible option types:
- change the deliberation template
- add a sidecar artifact type
- add workflow surfacing rules
- add a lightweight trace/log mechanism
- add review or challenge checkpoints
- add community / submission pathways
- deliberately leave some concerns outside the formal apparatus and preserve them through practice instead

Outputs:
- concrete apparatus-change options
- why each option may help
- what each option distorts or domesticates
- recommendation on whether the apparatus should change at all

### Layer 8: Research / Reference Layer

Research can be used, but not naively.

Questions:
- Are there useful reference designs or research traditions relevant to the deliberation?
- What do they actually support?
- What assumptions do they import that may not transfer here?
- Where would importing them reproduce the wrong institutional logic?

Rules:
- Prefer primary sources where feasible
- Treat them as structured provocations, not final authority
- Record what the reference design misses in this context

Outputs:
- bounded reference designs
- what they illuminate
- what would be dangerous to import uncritically

## Review Deliverable Shape

The final review should have:

1. Findings first
   - ordered by severity / leverage
   - with file references

2. For each deliberation
   - what it does well
   - what it misses
   - what kind of design stance it is advancing
   - what the current form could not hold

3. Cross-deliberation synthesis
   - where the four reinforce each other
   - where they duplicate
   - where they should remain separate
   - what larger framework direction they imply

4. Roadmap integration map
   - modify existing phase
   - insert new phase
   - defer to next milestone
   - or keep as open orientation only

5. Apparatus-change options
   - not just "fit to current system"
   - but "how should the system change, if at all, to meet this demand?"

## Current Cautions

- Do not let the current GSD artifact vocabulary dictate the final answer.
- Do not treat philosophical language as noise; translate it into design pressure without stripping its force.
- Do not assume a framework concern is automatically phase-worthy.
- Do not assume every deep orientation should become a workflow feature.
- Do not confuse "important" with "ready to operationalize."
- Do not let one active user's habits silently become framework law.
- Do preserve what exceeded the form, even if the final recommendation is to keep that excess outside the formal system.
