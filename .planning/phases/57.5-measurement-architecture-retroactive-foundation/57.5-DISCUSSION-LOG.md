## Preamble: why this discuss-phase was handoff-driven

This context pass did not start from live gray-area questioning. The project already had a dense authority chain telling us what the next step was and what evidence had to ground it:

1. `pre-57.5-handoff.md` explicitly named `/gsdr:discuss-phase 57.5` as the critical-path next action.
2. The deliberation `measurement-infrastructure-epistemic-foundations.md` had already fixed the architectural direction and phase split.
3. The 2026-04-16 correction chain (`correction-and-extensions`, `anomaly-stress-tests`, `e5.8`, spike 009, spike 010) had already invalidated several earlier assumptions about source classification and reasoning metrics.

So this discuss-phase was a synthesis-and-steering pass, not an open-ended brainstorming interview. The job here was to turn the deliberation plus audit evidence into a planning-ready steering brief without reopening settled questions or silently narrowing the phase again.

## Claim Justifications

### Three-layer architecture and source families

**[decided:reasoned] 57.5 should implement the measurement system as three explicit layers**
- **Alternatives considered:** keep extending telemetry ad hoc; dashboard-first buildout
- **Why rejected:** ad hoc telemetry extension recreates the Phase 57 narrowing; dashboard-first skips the substrate the roadmap says must exist first
- **Authority:** `.planning/deliberations/measurement-infrastructure-epistemic-foundations.md` Sections 3-5

**[decided:reasoned] Raw-source families must be explicit from day one**
- **Alternatives considered:** flatten all sources into a common "measurement source" bucket
- **Why rejected:** E5/E5.8 showed `/insights` artifacts have distinct lifecycle/write-path/coverage behavior that query consumers must not mistake for native runtime truth
- **Citation:** `.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md` MEAS-DERIVED-01..06, `.planning/audits/2026-04-15-measurement-signal-inventory/e5.8-insights-experiment/RESULTS.md`

**[evidenced:cited] `telemetry.cjs` and `kb.cjs` are reusable substrate anchors**
- **Citation:** `get-shit-done/bin/lib/telemetry.cjs`, `get-shit-done/bin/lib/kb.cjs`
- **Verification:** Read directly during this resume pass; both already implement reusable loading/build patterns but stop short of a shared registry/query layer

### Loop coverage and extractor priorities

**[decided:reasoned] 57.5 leads with intervention-lifecycle + pipeline-integrity loops**
- **Alternatives considered:** broad all-loops scope now; narrow telemetry-only continuation
- **Why rejected:** all-loops scope belongs to 57.6/57.7; telemetry-only continuation repeats the original scope-loss failure
- **Authority:** Phase 57.5/57.6/57.7 roadmap entries and MEAS phase mapping in REQUIREMENTS.md

**[decided:reasoned] kb freshness repair belongs inside 57.5**
- **Alternatives considered:** leave freshness bug to a later maintenance task
- **Why rejected:** MEAS-GSDR-04 depends on fresh SQLite data; without the fix, the signal-quality side of the substrate is untrustworthy from day one
- **Citation:** `.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md` MEAS-GSDR-04/05, `.planning/audits/2026-04-15-measurement-signal-inventory/anomaly-stress-tests.md` E2

**[assumed:reasoned] One low-risk Codex extractor is the right 57.5 proof**
- **Challenge protocol:** If no low-ambiguity Codex extractor materially serves the 57.5 loops, the phase may need either a broader Codex slice or a narrower proof criterion
- **Evidence checked:** Roadmap requires at least one Codex extractor; current evidence chain says full parity is not 57.5's job
- **Why reasonable:** Architectural proof is the minimum needed to prevent a Claude-first design from hardening

### Query and interpretation model

**[decided:reasoned] Query layer should return interpretation objects, not bare metrics**
- **Alternatives considered:** numeric/statistical outputs only
- **Why rejected:** the deliberation explicitly grounds the system in competing readings, distinguishing features, anomaly tracking, and epistemic provenance rather than verification-style scores
- **Authority:** `measurement-infrastructure-epistemic-foundations.md` Sections 2-4

**[decided:reasoned] 57.5 only implements epistemic machinery v1**
- **Alternatives considered:** ship intervention histories and revision classification immediately
- **Why rejected:** those need the substrate first; pushing them into 57.5 would overload the phase and blur the 57.6/57.7 split
- **Authority:** roadmap phase split plus the deliberation's own staged breakdown

### Corpus boundaries and reasoning signals

**[evidenced:cited] JSONL is canonical for token totals and human-turn extraction**
- **Citation:** `.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md` MEAS-RUNTIME-03/04/09/10, `.planning/audits/2026-04-15-measurement-signal-inventory/anomaly-stress-tests.md` E3/E4
- **Verification:** correction chain explicitly reclassified session-meta token semantics as unreliable and pushed JSONL forward as canonical

**[decided:reasoned] Reasoning-quality measurement is out of 57.5 scope**
- **Alternatives considered:** adopt summary length or marker densities as a quality proxy; ignore reasoning seams entirely
- **Why rejected:** spike 010's qualitative comparison falsified the length-as-quality shortcut; ignoring it entirely would leave no clean future seam
- **Citation:** `.planning/spikes/010-parent-session-thinking-summary-proxy/DECISION.md`, `qualitative_comparison.md`

**[decided:reasoned] `/insights` products should be modeled as DERIVED with explicit provenance**
- **Alternatives considered:** continue treating `session-meta` and `facets` as runtime telemetry
- **Why rejected:** empirical /insights testing showed two-path writes, strict incremental behavior, and biased facets coverage
- **Citation:** `e5.8-insights-experiment/RESULTS.md`, `anomaly-stress-tests.md` E5/E5.8
