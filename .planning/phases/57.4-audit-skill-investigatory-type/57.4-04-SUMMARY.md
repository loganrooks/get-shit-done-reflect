### Tone-guidance encounters

Three places where the spec could have read as "checklist" and was rewritten to read as "engagement":

1. **Execution section step 3** ("Engage with each obligation in turn") — initially drafted as "For each obligation, check whether it applies, then address it." Rewritten to frame obligations as constraints on attention that shape what counts as a finding, with specific per-orientation entry points (I1 discrepancy, initiating question, standard subject obligations as engagement contract).
2. **Standard-orientation scaffold "Findings" subsection** — resisted the temptation to prescribe a findings table format. The prose says "each finding separates evidence from interpretation — the citation is one thing, what the citation means is another, and they must not be collapsed," which is a practice instruction, not a format requirement.
3. **Investigatory scaffold "How I Navigated Tensions Between Obligations"** — the final sentence was added deliberately: "If no tensions emerged, name that too and consider whether the absence is a sign the obligations were under-engaged with." This mirrors the anti-performativity warning applied to Rule 5 and to the excess section — absence is itself a signal that deserves interrogation, not a neutral result.
