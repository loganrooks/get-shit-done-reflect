## Accomplishments

### Section 3 — Audit Taxonomy: Three Orthogonal Axes (rewritten)

Replaced the flat 8-type enum with the 3-axis decomposition. The new section has four subsections:

- **Preamble** — Names what the v1 enum conflated (subject × stance × delegation under one field) and why templates couldn't compose across the conflation. Cites `audit-taxonomy-three-axis-obligations.md`, not the pre-rewrite Section 3. States that subject is optional under investigatory/exploratory and that the axes themselves are provisional per DC-5 and forms-excess.
- **Axis 1 — Subject** — 9-row subject table with `Family | What it examines` columns, followed immediately by the 9-row epistemic profile table (`Oriented toward | Assumes | Might miss`). `process_review` and `artifact_analysis` flagged as NEW subjects from the retrospective. `cross_model_review` explicitly documented as exited, with the user's verbatim quote cited.
- **Axis 2 — Orientation** — 3-row table (standard/investigatory/exploratory), plus prose on the orthogonal character (any orientation × any subject) and the hermeneutic distinction per CONTEXT.md G-5 (investigatory occasioned by breakdown, exploratory by possibility; relationship between them deliberately not ontologically settled).
- **Axis 3 — Delegation** — 2-row table (self / cross_model) with cross-model fragility stated factually from the deliberation line 100.
- **Section 3.4 — The escape hatch (preserved and extended)** — Value-level (subject omitted under investigatory/exploratory) and axis-level (per DC-5, the decomposition itself is subject to excess), with the forms-excess quote as governing authority.

### Section 4 — Output Paradigm: Obligations and Templates (rewritten)

Replaced the per-family body templates with the two-populations framing:

- **4.1 Two populations** — Population 1 (standard routine, templates as scaffolding) and Population 2 (investigatory/exploratory/complex, obligations replace templates because they compose). Complexity assessment explicitly delegated to the orchestrator (Plan 03), not encoded here. Population 3 (multi-agent) deferred per CONTEXT.md Q3.
- **4.2 Obligations vs. templates — the difference that matters** — Written as a hermeneutic distinction per deliberation lines 111–125. Templates say "write a section"; obligations say "you must address X". Why this is the shift that lets obligations compose across axes. "What the Obligations Didn't Capture" named as mandatory in every audit output under both paradigms, with the feedback loop into taxonomy revision.
- **4.3 Suggested scaffolding for standard-orientation audits** — Three scaffolds retained (formerly v1 structural/epistemic/compliance), now attributed by subject, each explicitly marked as "suggested for standard orientation only" with "What the Obligations Didn't Capture" added as a mandatory section inside each. `process_review` and `artifact_analysis` deliberately left without scaffolds. V1 exploratory template removed (exploratory is now an orientation).
- **4.4 Obligation sets live in audit-ground-rules.md** — Cross-reference to the file Plan 02 is rewriting in parallel, listing what lives there (core rules, orientation obligations, subject obligations, cross-cutting obligations, composition principle).

## Decisions & Deviations

### Deliberate Decisions

- **Family column retained as historical label, not authoritative typology.** V1 families (structural/epistemic/compliance) were interpretive clustering and the new subjects don't fit them. Kept in the Subject column for readers familiar with v1, explicitly deauthorized as a typology in the prose.
- **`process_review` and `artifact_analysis` scaffolds deliberately unspecified.** Per Claude's Discretion in CONTEXT.md — scaffolds will emerge from practice. Inventing scaffolds absent usage data would be speculation.
- **Section 5 Layer 3 touched beyond minimal edit.** The plan permitted "a one-word-level edit" on Layer 2 prose but was silent on Layer 3. Layer 3 contained literal `audit_type: exploratory` which Section 2's rewrite made a silent contradiction. Fixed the contradiction and extended Layer 3 per DC-5 to note that escape now reaches the axes themselves. Scope expansion was semantic-consistency-driven, not ambitious — leaving the contradiction would have been the failure mode this phase exists to prevent.
- **`ground_rules` value grammar reframed inline rather than deferred.** The plan flagged this as the most fragile touch and allowed a "(value grammar under revision)" marker as a fallback. The full reframe was chosen because Plan 03 (rewriting `/gsdr:audit`) will need to emit values matching whatever grammar Section 2 documents, and a marker would have propagated the ambiguity. New grammar: `core+orientation+subject` (e.g., `core+investigatory+process_review`, `core+exploratory` when subject omitted).

### Deviations from Plan

**None at the task level** — all three tasks executed per the plan's `<action>` sections. Two authorial decisions (Layer 3 expansion, `ground_rules` inline reframe) are within the plan's discretion clauses for handling semantic consistency.

### Scope-Ambiguity Encounters

- **Section 5 Layer 3** — the plan's "Do NOT touch" list omitted Layer 3. Strict reading would have preserved `audit_type: exploratory` literally. Chose consistency fix because the plan's second-order check principle (`sig-2026-03-01-plan-checker-misses-second-order-effects`) explicitly names silent-contradiction prevention as the executor's responsibility. Documented here so Plan 06 or the verifier can audit the decision.
- **`ground_rules` value grammar** — the plan listed this as the "most fragile touch" and authorized either full reframe or a marker. Chose full reframe for the reasons above.
