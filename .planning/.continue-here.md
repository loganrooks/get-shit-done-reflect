## Epistemic Context for Next Milestone Planning

### What v1.18 Revealed

The 12-day v1.18 sync was the fork's first structured upstream adoption. The retrospective (`.planning/phases/54-sync-retrospective-governance/54-RETROSPECTIVE.md`) identified:

**What worked:** Baseline-freeze (prevented scope creep), modular adoption (incremental phases 45-48), deep integration standard (Phase 53 wove features into pipeline), research-informed planning.

**What didn't work:** Drift management (upstream moved 372 commits during sync), scope discovery (10 phases emerged from what started as ~5), progress telemetry (STATE.md overstated completion).

**5 sync-round issues needing future attention:** Scope revision protocol, squash merge risk, PR workflow overhead, deliberation tracking across sessions, quick task sprawl.

These represent unresolved design questions that should inform next milestone scoping:

1. **`sensor-expansion-and-harness-integration-gaps.md`** — Option E (research-informed harness evolution) proposed. Core question: should the harness become more reflexive about its own limitations? Directly relevant to M-B (Meta-Observability).

   - **M-B: Meta-Observability** — Log sensor, session telemetry, observation completeness. The harness can't observe its own blind spots.
